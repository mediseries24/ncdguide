Global NCD Protocols

  • 🌐 WHO PEN Guidelines (Primary Care)
  • Cardiovascular Risk Management
    • CVD Risk < 10%: Lifestyle modification (diet, exercise, smoking cessation).
    • CVD Risk 10-20%: Monitor every 3-6 months. Consider Statin if LDL remains high.
    • CVD Risk > 20%: Start BP lowering drugs + Statin (Atorvastatin 20mg) + Low-dose Aspirin (if no contraindications).
  • Hypertension (Stepwise Approach)
    • Target BP: < 140/90 mmHg (Standard) / < 130/80 mmHg (Diabetic/CKD).
    • Step 1: ACEi (Enalapril) OR ARB (Losartan) OR CCB (Amlodipine).
    • Step 2: Combine ACEi/ARB + CCB.
    • Step 3: Add Thiazide Diuretic (HCTZ 12.5mg).
    • Note for Black patients: CCB or Thiazide is preferred as first-line.
  • Type 2 Diabetes Management
    • Target HbA1c: < 7.0% (individualized). Fasting Sugar < 7.0 mmol/L.
    • 1st Line: Metformin (up to 2000mg/day). Monitor eGFR.
    • 2nd Line: Add Sulfonylurea (Gliclazide) or DPP-4 Inhibitor.
    • If Uncontrolled (HbA1c > 9%): Initiate Basal Insulin (10 Units at bedtime) and titrate slowly.
  • ⚕️ MSF Clinical Guidelines (Resource-Limited Settings)
  • Asthma in Adults & Children
    • Mild Intermittent: SABA (Salbutamol inhaler) PRN.
    • Mild Persistent: Low-dose ICS (Beclomethasone/Budesonide) + SABA PRN.
    • Moderate-Severe: ICS + LABA (Formoterol) + oral Prednisolone for acute exacerbations (40-50mg/day for 5-7 days).
    • Always prescribe a spacer for children and elderly.
  • Epilepsy Management
    • Focal & Generalized Seizures: Sodium Valproate or Carbamazepine.
    • Alternative/Resource-poor: Phenobarbital (cheap, effective, but sedating).
    • Do not stop AED abruptly (risk of status epilepticus).
  • Severe Hypoglycemia (Emergency)
    • Conscious Patient: Oral fast-acting sugar (fruit juice, sugar water).
    • Unconscious Patient: IV 10% or 50% Glucose / Dextrose stat. Followed by 5% Dextrose infusion if needed.
  • 📚 NCD Foundation & Advanced Protocols
  • Chronic Kidney Disease (CKD) with DM/HTN
    • Microalbuminuria screening: Annually for all DM/HTN patients.
    • Renoprotection: ACEi or ARB is mandatory if Urine ACR is high, even if BP is normal.
    • Drug adjustment: Stop Metformin if eGFR < 30 mL/min.
  • Post-Myocardial Infarction (Secondary Prevention)
    All patients post-MI should be on the "Big 4" (unless contraindicated):
    1. Dual Antiplatelet Therapy (DAPT) - Aspirin + Clopidogrel.
    2. High-intensity Statin (Atorvastatin 40-80mg).
    3. Beta-Blocker (Bisoprolol/Metoprolol).
    4. ACE Inhibitor or ARB.
  • COPD (GOLD Guidelines Summary)
    • Group A (Low risk, fewer symptoms): SABA or SAMA PRN.
    • Group B (Low risk, more symptoms): LAMA (Tiotropium) or LABA daily.
    • Group C/D (High risk/Exacerbations): LAMA + LABA or LABA + ICS.
    • Vaccinations: Annual Influenza and Pneumococcal vaccine recommended.

Complete Rx Templates

  • 1. Hypertension (Newly Diagnosed)
    Rx:
    - Tab. Amlodipine 5mg (1+0+0) After breakfast x 30 Days
    Or (If Diabetic/Proteinuria):
    - Tab. Losartan 50mg (1+0+0) After breakfast x 30 Days
    Advice: Low salt diet (<5g/day), Daily brisk walk for 30 mins.
  • 2. Type 2 Diabetes (Initial)
    Rx:
    - Tab. Metformin 500mg (0+0+1) After dinner x 7 Days (To avoid GI upset)
    - Then increase to (1+0+1) After meal x 30 Days
    Advice: Avoid sugar/sweets. Diabetic diet chart follow. Check Fasting Blood Sugar (FBS) after 14 days.
  • 3. Type 2 Diabetes (Uncontrolled on Metformin)
    Rx:
    - Tab. Sitagliptin + Metformin (50/500mg) (1+0+1) After meal x 30 Days
    Advice: Regular eye and foot care. Check HbA1c every 3 months.
  • 4. Asthma (Maintenance)
    Rx:
    - Inhaler Budesonide + Formoterol (200/6mcg)
    Take 2 Puffs twice daily (Morning & Night) x Continue
    - Inhaler Salbutamol (100mcg) 2 puffs SOS (Only during acute attack)
    Advice: Wash mouth after using steroid inhaler. Avoid dust and cold.
  • 5. COPD (Stable)
    Rx:
    - Inhaler Tiotropium (18mcg) 1 Puff once daily x Continue
    Advice: Stop Smoking immediately. Breathing exercises.
  • 6. Dyslipidemia (High Cholesterol)
    Rx:
    - Tab. Atorvastatin 20mg (0+0+1) After dinner / Bedtime x 3 Months
    Advice: Avoid red meat, fast food, and saturated fats.
  • 7. Ischemic Heart Disease (Post-MI / Angina)
    Rx:
    - Tab. Aspirin 75mg (0+1+0) After lunch x Continue
    - Tab. Clopidogrel 75mg (1+0+0) After breakfast x 1 Year
    - Tab. Bisoprolol 2.5mg (1+0+0) After breakfast x Continue
    - Tab. Atorvastatin 40mg (0+0+1) Bedtime x Continue
    - Spray Glyceryl Trinitrate (GTN) 1-2 puffs under tongue SOS (For severe chest pain)

Diet & Lifestyle Guide

  • DASH Diet (Hypertension)
    • Sodium Limit: < 2.3g/day (Ideal: < 1.5g/day). Avoid extra table salt.
    • Increase: Potassium-rich foods (Banana, Spinach, Potatoes) *if kidneys are normal*.
    • Avoid: Processed foods, pickles, fast food, and canned soups.
  • Diabetic Diet (Low GI)
    • Plate Method: 50% Non-starchy veg (greens), 25% Lean Protein (fish/chicken), 25% Complex Carbs (brown rice/whole wheat).
    • Avoid: Refined sugar, sweets, cold drinks, white flour (maida).
    • Fruits: 1-2 servings/day (Apple, Guava, Papaya). Avoid fruit juices.
  • Renal Diet (CKD Patients)
    • Protein: Restrict to 0.6-0.8 g/kg/day (unless on dialysis).
    • Potassium Restriction: Avoid bananas, coconut water, tomatoes, citrus fruits.
    • Fluid Limit: As per nephrologist (usually 1 - 1.5 L/day if edema present).
  • Lipid-Lowering Diet
    • Fats: Use Olive/Mustard/Sunflower oil. Avoid Palm oil, Dalda, Ghee, Butter.
    • Avoid: Red meat (Beef/Mutton), egg yolk (limit to 2/week), deep-fried foods.
    • Increase: Omega-3 rich fish, oats, nuts (almonds/walnuts).
  • General Lifestyle
    • Exercise: At least 150 mins of moderate aerobic exercise per week (e.g., 30 mins brisk walk, 5 days/week).
    • Habits: Strict cessation of Smoking and smokeless tobacco (Jorda/Gul). Stop alcohol.

Lab Investigations & Normal Values

  • 🩸 Glycemic Profile
    • Fasting Blood Sugar (FBS): Normal < 5.6 mmol/L | Diabetic ≥ 7.0 mmol/L
    • 2-Hours ABF (PPBS): Normal < 7.8 mmol/L | Diabetic ≥ 11.1 mmol/L
    • HbA1c: Normal < 5.7% | Pre-DM 5.7-6.4% | DM ≥ 6.5%
  • 🫀 Fasting Lipid Profile (FLP)
    • Total Cholesterol: < 200 mg/dL (Desirable)
    • Triglycerides (TG): < 150 mg/dL
    • LDL (Bad Chol): < 100 mg/dL (Target < 70 mg/dL for high CVD risk)
    • HDL (Good Chol): > 40 mg/dL (Male) | > 50 mg/dL (Female)
  • 🧪 Renal Function Test (RFT)
    • Serum Creatinine: 0.6 - 1.2 mg/dL
    • Blood Urea Nitrogen (BUN): 7 - 20 mg/dL
    • Urine ACR (Microalbumin): Normal < 30 mg/g | High 30-300 mg/g
    • Serum Potassium (K+): 3.5 - 5.0 mEq/L
  • 🔬 Hepatic & Others
    • SGPT (ALT) / SGOT (AST): Generally < 40 U/L
    • Serum Uric Acid: 3.5 - 7.2 mg/dL (Gout target < 6.0 mg/dL)
    • TSH (Thyroid): 0.4 - 4.0 mIU/L

Routine Follow-up Protocols

  • Diabetes Care Routine
    • Every Visit (1-3 months): Check BP, Weight/BMI, Fasting/Random Glucose, check feet for ulcers.
    • Every 3-6 months: Check HbA1c (if not at target or therapy changed).
    • Annually (The Big 3):
    1. Eye: Dilated Retinopathy screening.
    2. Kidney: UACR & Serum Creatinine (eGFR).
    3. Nerves: Comprehensive Foot Exam (Monofilament test).
  • Hypertension Follow-up
    • Newly Diagnosed / Dose Changed: Review every 2-4 weeks until target BP is achieved.
    • Stable Patients: Review every 3-6 months.
    • Annual Labs: Lipid Profile, Serum Creatinine, Electrolytes (Na+, K+), ECG, Urine Routine.
  • Asthma & COPD Protocol
    • 1-3 Months: Assess symptom control (night awakenings, rescue inhaler use).
    • Every Visit: Check inhaler technique and adherence.
    • Annually: Spirometry check, Influenza vaccine (yearly), Pneumococcal vaccine (as per age/guideline).
  • IHD / Post-MI Follow-up
    • 1-3 Months: Review symptoms of angina/heart failure, ensure adherence to dual antiplatelets and statins.
    • Annually: Fasting Lipid Profile (Ensure LDL is at strict target), ECG, Echocardiogram (if HF symptoms present).
